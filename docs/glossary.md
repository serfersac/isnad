
# Glossary

## Inscription
**Definition**: The process of recording AI resources (such as skills, configurations, prompts, memory, and models) directly on the Base L2 blockchain. In ISNAD, inscriptions are permanent, censorship-resistant records that include both metadata and the actual content of the resource, ensuring authenticity and verifiability without external dependencies.

## Attestation
**Definition**: A formal declaration by an auditor that a specific AI resource is safe and trustworthy. In ISNAD, auditors stake $ISNAD tokens to attest to the safety of resources, locking their stake for a specified period. Attestations are pinned to specific resource versions, ensuring that only verified versions are considered trustworthy.

## Slashing
**Definition**: A penalty mechanism where a portion or all of an auditor's staked $ISNAD tokens are forfeited if they provide incorrect attestations or if a resource they attested to is found to be malicious. Slashing incentivizes honest behavior and deters malicious actions by making auditors financially accountable for their attestations.

## Trust Tier
**Definition**: A classification system that categorizes AI resources based on the amount of $ISNAD staked by auditors, the number of independent auditors, and the duration of the attestation lock period. Trust Tiers in ISNAD include UNVERIFIED, REVIEWED, VERIFIED, TRUSTED, and CERTIFIED, with higher tiers indicating greater trustworthiness and security.

## Auditor Jury
**Definition**: A group of randomly selected auditors who review and vote on flagged resources. When a resource is flagged as potentially malicious, an Auditor Jury is convened to determine the resource's status. Each juror votes on whether the resource is MALICIOUS, CLEAN, or ABSTAIN, with a supermajority required for a verdict. This multi-layered detection system ensures fairness and accountability in the trust assessment process.
